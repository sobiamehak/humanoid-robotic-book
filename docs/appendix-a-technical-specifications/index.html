Appendix A - Technical Specifications and Standards

Hardware Specifications​

Common Humanoid Robot Platforms​

Honda ASIMO​

  • Height: 130 cm
  • Weight: 48 kg
  • Degrees of Freedom: 57 (2 legs: 12 DOF each, 2 arms: 12 DOF each, 2 hands: 12 DOF each, head: 7 DOF)
  • Maximum Walking Speed: 1 km/h (slow), 6 km/h (fast)
  • Sensors: Multiple cameras, force sensors in feet, gyroscope, accelerometer
  • Power System: Rechargeable battery with approximately 1-hour operation time

SoftBank Robotics Pepper​

  • Height: 120 cm
  • Weight: 28 kg
  • Degrees of Freedom: 20 (Head: 2, Arms: 16, Wheels: 2)
  • Sensors: 3 cameras, 3 microphones, 1 touch sensor (head), 8 ultrasonic sensors, 1 infrared LED
  • Power System: 12 hours of battery life with 12-hour charging time
  • Connectivity: Wi-Fi, 4G (optional)

Aldebaran NAO​

  • Height: 58 cm
  • Weight: 5.2 kg
  • Degrees of Freedom: 25
  • Sensors: 2 cameras, 4 microphones, 1 inertial measurement unit, 2 sonars, 1 tactile sensor, 8 force sensors
  • Actuators: 25 servo motors
  • Power System: 90 minutes battery life

Boston Dynamics Atlas​

  • Height: 180 cm (without head), 190 cm (with head)
  • Weight: 80 kg
  • Degrees of Freedom: 28 (6 in each arm, 6 in legs, 1 in torso, 3 in head)
  • Power System: Hydraulic power system with tethered operation for most demonstrations
  • Sensors: Stereo vision, LIDAR, IMU, force sensors

Common Sensors and Specifications​

Cameras​

  • Resolution: Commonly 640x480 to 1920x1080 pixels
  • Frame Rate: 30-60 fps for real-time applications
  • Field of View: 60-120 degrees depending on lens
  • Types: RGB, depth (RGB-D), stereo vision

IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit)​

  • Accelerometer Range: ±2g to ±16g
  • Gyroscope Range: ±250°/s to ±2000°/s
  • Magnetometer: Often included for absolute orientation
  • Update Rate: 100-1000 Hz

Force/Torque Sensors​

  • Load Capacity: 10N to 1000N depending on application
  • Accuracy: 0.1% to 2% of full scale
  • Update Rate: 100-1000 Hz
  • Axes: 3-axis (force) or 6-axis (force + torque)

Joint Actuators​

  • Torque Range: 0.5 Nm to 100+ Nm depending on location
  • Speed: 0.1 to 10 rad/s continuous
  • Resolution: 0.1° to 0.01° depending on encoder

Software Specifications​

Operating Systems​

  • ROS (Robot Operating System): Version 1 (Kinetic, Melodic) and Version 2 (Foxy, Humble)
  • Real-time Capabilities: Often achieved through Xenomai or RT kernel patches
  • Programming Languages: C++, Python, Java, MATLAB

Middleware​

  • Communication Protocols: TCP/IP, UDP, CAN bus
  • Message Formats: Messages in bag files, TF transforms
  • Real-time Requirements: Deterministic communication with <1ms latency for control loops
  • Security: Transport layer security, authentication mechanisms

Cognitive Systems​

  • ML Frameworks: TensorFlow, PyTorch, Caffe, OpenCV
  • Processing Power: Typically 2-8 core ARM or x86 processors with GPU acceleration
  • Memory: 2GB to 32GB RAM depending on application
  • Storage: 16GB to 1TB depending on data requirements

Safety Standards and Regulations​

International Standards​

ISO 13482:2014 - Personal Care Robots​

  • Scope: Safety requirements for personal care robots that physically interact with users
  • Key Requirements:
    • Risk assessment and mitigation procedures
    • Safety-related control system requirements
    • Design requirements for protection measures
    • Verification and validation procedures
    • Information for use including warnings and instructions

ISO 12100:2010 - Safety of Machinery​

  • Scope: Fundamental principles for risk assessment and risk reduction
  • Key Elements:
    • Risk analysis and risk evaluation
    • Risk reduction hierarchy (inherently safe design, safeguards, information)
    • Integration of safety considerations in design process

ISO 10218-1 and -2 - Industrial Robots​

  • Part 1: Safety requirements for industrial robots
  • Part 2: Safety requirements for robot systems and integration
  • Key Provisions: Safety-rated monitoring of collaborative operations, safety functions for hand-guiding, power and force limiting

Industry-Specific Standards​

IEC 62061 - Industrial Machines Safety​

  • Application: Functional safety of electrical, electronic, and programmable control systems
  • SIL Requirements: Typically SIL 2 or 3 for human-interactive robots

ISO 14121-1 - Risk Assessment​

  • Principles: Risk estimation, risk evaluation, risk reduction
  • Application: Foundation for robot safety standards

Regional Regulations​

European Union - CE Marking​

  • Machinery Directive 2006/42/EC: Applies to robots as machines
  • EMC Directive: Ensures electromagnetic compatibility
  • Low Voltage Directive: Safety requirements for electrical equipment
  • Radio Equipment Directive: For robots with wireless communication

United States - FDA Regulations​

  • For Medical Robots: Classification as medical devices
  • Premarket Approval (PMA): For high-risk devices
  • 510(k) Clearance: For devices substantially equivalent to existing devices

Japan - Safety Standards​

  • JIS Standards: Japanese Industrial Standards for robotics
  • SIP (Smart Interaction Programs): Government initiative for robotics safety

Communication Protocols and Interfaces​

Robot Communication Protocols​

ROS (Robot Operating System) Communication​

  • Transport Layer: TCPROS and UDPROS
  • Message Serialization: ROS Message (ROSMSG) format
  • Service Architecture: Request-response pattern for synchronous communication
  • Action Architecture: Feedback-enabled goal-based communication

Robot Framework Standards​

  • OMNIORB: Object Management Group standard for CORBA
  • YARP (Yet Another Robot Platform): Lightweight communication between robot components
  • ROS 2 DDS: Data Distribution System for real-time communication

Human-Robot Interface Standards​

  • ISO 9241-110: Ergonomic principles for human-robot interaction
  • ISO 12100: Safety requirements for machinery, adapted for HRI
  • IEC 62565-1: Visual display device properties for robot interfaces

Performance Benchmarks​

Locomotion Benchmarks​

  • Walking Speed: Measured in m/s (e.g., 0.3 m/s for stable walking)
  • Step Height: Maximum obstacle clearance during walking
  • Step Width: Minimum turning radius capability
  • Balance Recovery: Time to recover from disturbances

Manipulation Benchmarks​

  • Position Accuracy: End-effector position accuracy (e.g., <1cm)
  • Load Capacity: Maximum weight that can be manipulated
  • Dexterity: Tasks per minute (TPM) for specific tasks
  • Grasp Success Rate: Percentage of successful object grasps

Interaction Benchmarks​

  • Speech Recognition Accuracy: Percentage correctly recognized commands
  • Response Time: Time from stimulus to robot response
  • Task Completion Rate: Percentage of tasks completed successfully
  • User Satisfaction: Measured through standardized questionnaires

Manufacturing and Maintenance Specifications​

Materials and Manufacturing​

  • Enclosures: Typically ABS, polycarbonate, or carbon fiber composites
  • Joining Methods: Snap-fit, threaded fasteners, adhesives
  • Finishes: UV-resistant coatings, antimicrobial treatments
  • Tolerances: ±0.1mm to ±1mm depending on application

Maintenance Requirements​

  • Scheduled Maintenance: Visual inspection every 100-500 hours of operation
  • Lubrication Intervals: Every 500-1000 hours depending on joint usage
  • Calibration: Position and force sensor calibration every 3-6 months
  • Software Updates: Regular security and functionality updates

Testing and Validation Protocols​

Safety Testing​

  • Electrical Safety: IEC 60950-1 and IEC 61010-1 standards
  • EMC Testing: Immunity and emissions testing
  • Mechanical Safety: Torque limiting, speed limiting, emergency stops
  • Environmental Testing: Temperature, humidity, vibration tests

Performance Validation​

  • Functional Testing: Validation of all robot capabilities
  • Stress Testing: Operation under extreme but safe conditions
  • Endurance Testing: Long-term operation validation
  • Regression Testing: Ensuring updates don't break existing functionality

Quality Assurance​

  • Design Reviews: Formal reviews at key development milestones
  • Code Quality: Automated code analysis and review processes
  • Documentation Standards: Comprehensive technical and user documentation
  • Traceability: Requirements-to-implementation traceability matrices
